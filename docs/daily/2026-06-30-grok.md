## Hot Discussions

- **Loop engineering over manual prompting**: Strong theme that the high-leverage work is designing autonomous loops (event triggers, reflection, verification sub-agents, feedback writeback to Context Hub/memory) so agents run, learn, and improve between human interventions rather than requiring constant steering.

- **State isolation and observability to tame long-horizon complexity**: Widespread agreement that single monolithic agent contexts lead to pollution, high cost, and fragility; the shift to explicit business state models, subagent isolation, rich timelines, token/cost attribution, and trace-as-source-of-truth is required for debuggability and safe iteration at scale.

- **Evals + observability as the non-negotiable baseline for self-improvement**: Consensus that without systematic production trace sampling, targeted evals, and mechanisms (Engine or custom) to feed failures back into harness changes, agents stay static. Production reliability stems from architecture and continuous improvement infrastructure, not model selection alone.
